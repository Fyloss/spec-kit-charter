# Compliance rules

## Core Principles

- Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and organizational policies.
- Follow established security, privacy, and governance requirements by default.
- Apply the principle of least privilege to access, permissions, and data usage.
- Never expose, hardcode, or log secrets, credentials, or sensitive information.
- Collect, process, and retain only the minimum data required for the intended purpose.
- Ensure sensitive operations are auditable and traceable when appropriate.
- Respect data classification and handling requirements.
- Prefer approved libraries, services, and dependencies over unverified alternatives.
- Identify and communicate compliance risks, assumptions, and trade-offs.
- Do not bypass security, approval, or governance controls for convenience.
- Produce solutions that are maintainable, reviewable, and suitable for audit.

## Before Completing Any Change

- Verify compliance with applicable policies and standards.
- Check that no sensitive information is exposed.
- Confirm security and privacy requirements are satisfied.
- Ensure appropriate logging, auditing, and access controls are considered.
- Flag any uncertainty or compliance risk instead of making assumptions.
